What EV53 is
A regenerative protein, delivered where the body heals itself — at the cell membrane.

EV53 engineers a food-grade Lactobacillus strain to produce MG53-enriched extracellular vesicles: stable, absorbable, protein-loaded packages that act at the gut barrier with the potential for whole-body repair signaling. It is a scalable, low-cost way to deliver a regenerative biologic through a format the body already knows.

The active ingredient

MG53 — a natural molecular bandage

A human regenerative protein the body uses to repair cell membranes and resolve injury. Decades of peer-reviewed research underpin it.

The platform

How EV53 works

Four steps from a familiar probiotic to a delivered regenerative biologic.

1

Probiotic chassis

A food-grade Lactobacillus strain isolated from a centenarian source.

2

Gene-edited

Engineered to sharply boost vesicle output and load it with MG53.

3

MG53-rich EVs

Vesicles package the protein — stable, absorbable, lipid-protected cargo.

4

Delivered orally

Acts at the gut barrier, with potential for systemic repair signaling.

The result: a scalable, low-cost route to a regenerative biologic in a format people already reach for.

The evidence

Early data is compelling

Engineering MG53 into the strain does two things at once: it dramatically raises vesicle yield, and it loads those vesicles with a regenerative payload.

Dramatically higher EV yield

MG53 engineering enhances vesicle output many-fold — the foundation for scalable, low-cost production.

Gut protection in disease models

MG53-secreting Lactobacillus reduced gut-barrier permeability and preserved colon length in colitis models.

Metabolic & recovery potential

Vesicles are enriched in cardiolipins, linked to mitochondrial and metabolic support.

Peer-reviewed science

The research behind MG53

Decades of independent, peer-reviewed work underpin the platform. Each title links to its source.

MG53 protects against intestinal inflammation by inhibiting NLRP3 inflammasome activation ↗
Li et al. · Frontiers in Pharmacology · 2026
Direct gut-health evidence: MG53 preserved the colonic barrier and calmed inflammasome-driven inflammation.
MG53 nucleates assembly of cell membrane repair machinery ↗
Cai et al. · Nature Cell Biology · 2009
The foundational paper identifying MG53 as a core organizer of cell-membrane repair.
Sustained elevation of MG53 increases tissue regenerative capacity ↗
Bian et al. · Nature Communications · 2019
Systemic MG53 boosted regeneration across tissues — with no adverse effect on glucose or metabolism.
Recombinant MG53 modulates therapeutic cell-membrane repair in muscular dystrophy ↗
Weisleder et al. · Science Translational Medicine · 2012
Translational proof that recombinant MG53 works as an applied protein to restore membrane integrity.
MG53 suppresses NF-κB activation to mitigate age-related heart failure ↗
Wang et al. · JCI Insight · 2021
Shows MG53's broader anti-inflammatory, tissue-protective reach beyond the gut.

MG53 evidence is preclinical (cell, animal, and topical studies). Claims describe documented biological activity, not approved health claims.
